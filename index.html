Welcome to my personal page! I am currently a senior undergraduate in the School of Psychology, Beijing Normal University.

Academic Philosophy

Growing up in a rural Miao (Hmong) community, I rarely encountered formal mental-health services. Most families relied on traditional shamanistic practices, which provided some support but could not fully address mental-health needs.

This early exposure inspired me to study psychology at Beijing Normal University (BNU) and later engage in volunteer work across minority communities in Xinjiang, Tibet, and Inner Mongolia. There, I gained a clearer understanding of some structural barriers underlying the mental-health gap: insufficient funding, a shortage of well-trained providers, and limited cultural adaptation.

These observations now guide my research interests and fuel my commitment to exploring innovative digital approaches to improve the adaptability and scalability of mental health services.

I believe that emerging technologies, particularly artificial intelligence, provide unprecedented opportunities to address the global shortage of mental health services.

Research Interests

AI-Assisted Therapist Training

Using large language models to create low-risk practice clients for novice counselors, with real-time feedback and culturally tailored scenarios.

Therapeutic Process Analysis

AI-assisted qualitative analysis to identify therapist-client interaction patterns and predict treatment outcomes using multilevel modeling.

Neural Mechanisms of Learning and Synchrony

Exploring inter-brain synchrony and physiological coherence during therapy using fNIRS and HRV to understand alliance formation.

Cultural Integration in Mental Health

Integrating Cultural Formulation Interview (CFI)-based procedures into interventions and AI-driven cultural tailoring for diverse populations.

AI-Assisted Intervention Design

Exploring AI-assisted methods for culturally tailoring and evaluating intervention effectiveness in real-world settings.

Implementation & Intervention Science

Focusing on school-based and trauma-focused interventions, with emphasis on implementation science and scalability in underserved regions.

Long-term Career Goals

Clinical Scientist

Develop & validate evidence-based, culturally sensitive therapist training methods.

Educator & Mentor

Train & mentor next-generation mental-health professionals.

International Partner

Build global networks, especially for developing countries.

Tech Innovator

Create novel training tools to close the mental-health gap.

Conclusion

My academic journey began with a deep concern for the mental health needs of my rural Miao community. After rigorous training at Beijing Normal University, I am ready to pursue doctoral studies.

I am committed to integrating rigorous science with AI innovation to make mental health services more accessible, effective, and culturally responsive—especially for those who need them most.
