Welcome to my personal page! I am currently a senior undergraduate in the School of Psychology, Beijing Normal University.

Academic Philosophy

Growing up in a rural Miao (Hmong) community, I rarely encountered formal mental-health services. Most families relied on traditional shamanistic practices, which provided some support but could not fully address mental-health needs.

This early exposure inspired me to study psychology at Beijing Normal University (BNU) and later engage in volunteer work across minority communities in Xinjiang, Tibet, and Inner Mongolia. There, I gained a clearer understanding of some structural barriers underlying the mental-health gap: insufficient funding, a shortage of well-trained providers, and limited cultural adaptation.

These observations now guide my research interests and fuel my commitment to exploring innovative digital approaches to improve the adaptability and scalability of mental health services.

I believe that emerging technologies, particularly artificial intelligence, provide unprecedented opportunities to address the global shortage of mental health services.

Research Interests

AI-Assisted Therapist Training

The shortage of well-trained counselors remains a critical barrier to service delivery in underserved regions. I hope to develop scalable and low-cost training approaches to help reduce this gap.

In my future work, I plan to explore how AI can generate ecologically valid and diverse practice scenarios and create text- or voice-based AI-simulated clients for therapist skill development. I am also interested in designing real-time AI-based process evaluation and feedback systems to strengthen trainees’ competence and self-efficacy.

Psychotherapy Process Analysis

I plan to apply AI-assisted qualitative analysis and machine-learning methods to identify therapist–client interaction patterns that shape therapeutic processes and outcomes.

I also hope to use multilevel modeling and structural equation modeling to examine how these microprocesses explain variations in client outcomes, providing empirical foundations for targeted training module design.

Interpersonal Neurophysiological Synchrony

Building on my prior experience studying teacher–student neural synchrony, I plan to investigate therapist–client interpersonal synchrony as a mechanism underlying therapeutic alliance and outcomes. This work may involve collecting and analyzing HRV, EDA, and related physiological indicators.

By integrating neuroimaging and physiological measures, I hope to more comprehensively capture the dynamic interpersonal processes that facilitate therapeutic change and inform evidence-based counselor training.

Culturally Responsive Mental Health

I am interested in integrating Cultural Formulation Interview (CFI)-based assessments into psychotherapy and exploring how AI can support culturally tailored intervention design for diverse populations.

My future work will focus on improving cultural attunement in clinical decision-making and developing tools that help practitioners understand clients’ cultural contexts more systematically.

Implementation Science and Scalability

I plan to study the implementation and scalability of school-based and trauma-focused interventions, particularly in underserved or rural regions. My research will examine contextual barriers and facilitators that influence adoption and sustainability.

By integrating implementation-science frameworks with quantitative and qualitative methods, I hope to develop strategies that enhance intervention reach, fidelity, and long-term effectiveness.

I am committed to integrating rigorous science with AI innovation to make mental health services more accessible, effective, and culturally responsive—especially for those who need them most.
